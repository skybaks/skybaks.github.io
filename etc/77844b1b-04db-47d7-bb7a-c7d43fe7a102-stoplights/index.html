Stoplights

2026-07-08

Most of the time I spend annoyed while driving is either in highway traffic or at stoplights. My aggravation centers mainly around drivers who do not start moving off the line within a "reasonable" period of time after the light changes.

I will admit that my reactions are certainly not that of a racecar driver but I try to get moving withing at least 1 second of the light change and (if I'm really focused) sometimes within maybe half a second. I try to give people the benefit of the doubt but if its been 2 seconds I'm starting to get frustrated and if its been 3 or more seconds then I'm going to send out a terse BEEP to express my disaproval.

At most stoplights in the area where I do most of my driving, a delay of 2-4 seconds is not going to impact your ability to get across the intersection. Also, in the grand scheme of things, a delay of 2-4 seconds at a single stoplight translates to a delay of 2-4 arriving at your final destination. As meaningless as that might be, the impact it has on my mental state vastly overshadows the infantesimal timing impacts.

After coming to this conclusion, a healthy reaction might be to resolve my mind not to allow a 5 second delay to upset my mood. I guess I have some work to do.
